1. Custom Email Address Setup

Option A: Google Workspace (Recommended — $6/month)

Google Workspace gives you @prmlrecords.com email addresses that work exactly like Gmail — same app, same interface, just with your domain. You also get Google Calendar, Drive, Meet, and Docs for the team.

1
Go to workspace.google.com → click "Get Started" → enter prmlrecords.com as your domain
2
Follow the setup wizard. It will ask you to verify domain ownership by adding a TXT record to Namecheap. Log in to Namecheap → Domain List → Manage → Advanced DNS → Add New Record → Type: TXT → Host: @ → Value: the code Google gives you
3
Once verified, create your email accounts: hello@, support@, press@, info@, and any team member emails
4
Add the MX records Workspace gives you to Namecheap DNS. This routes your @prmlrecords.com email through Google's servers

Recommended Email Addresses to Create

  • hello@prmlrecords.com — general inquiries (your main address)
  • support@prmlrecords.com — customer support tickets
  • press@prmlrecords.com — media and press inquiries
  • partners@prmlrecords.com — partnership and B2B
  • community@prmlrecords.com — Creative UNDRGRND
  • fans@seaux9.com — SEAUX9 fan contact

Accessing Email on Phone and Computer

  • Computer: Go to mail.google.com and sign in with your @prmlrecords.com address
  • iPhone: Settings → Mail → Accounts → Add Account → Google → sign in with your Workspace email
  • Android: Open Gmail app → tap your profile photo → Add another account → Google → sign in

3. BIMI & Apple Branded Mail — Logo in Inbox

BIMI shows your logo next to your emails in Gmail, Apple Mail, and Yahoo. Clients see your brand before they even open the message.

Requirements Before You Start

  • You must have a domain-based email (done if you followed Section 1)
  • You need DMARC set up first (instructions below)
  • Your logo must be an SVG file in square format
  • For Gmail BIMI: you need a Verified Mark Certificate (VMC) from DigiCert (~$1,500/yr) OR use the free tier which works in some email clients
  • Apple Branded Mail is free and easier — no VMC required if your brand is established

Step 1: Set Up DMARC (Required First)

DMARC tells email providers what to do with emails that fail your authentication checks. You must set it up before BIMI will work.

Add this DNS record in Namecheap:

  • Type: TXT
  • Host: _dmarc
  • Value: v=DMARC1; p=quarantine; rua=mailto:dmarc@prmlrecords.com

Start with p=quarantine — this sends suspicious emails to spam instead of rejecting them. After 30 days with no issues, change to p=reject for maximum protection and BIMI eligibility.

Step 2: Set Up SPF and DKIM (Also Required)

Google Workspace sets these up for you automatically. Verify in Namecheap DNS:

  • SPF record (TXT, Host: @): v=spf1 include:_spf.google.com ~all
  • DKIM: Google Workspace admin panel → Apps → Gmail → Authenticate email → Generate DKIM key → Add to DNS

Step 3: Create Your BIMI Record

First, upload your logo as an SVG file to your website at: yoursite.com/bimi-logo.svg

Then add this DNS record in Namecheap:

  • Type: TXT
  • Host: default._bimi
  • Value: v=BIMI1; l=https://prmlrecords.com/bimi-logo.svg;

Timeline Expectations

  • Apple Branded Mail: ~2 weeks after DNS records are set and verified
  • BIMI in Gmail (free tier): 2–4 weeks, shows in some clients without VMC
  • BIMI with VMC (full Gmail display): 2–4 weeks after VMC is issued. Note: VMC requires logo to be in use 12+ months
  • Yahoo/AOL BIMI: 1–2 weeks, does not require VMC

4. Gmail Email Templates (Google Workspace)

In Gmail, you can save templates to reuse for common emails. Here is how:

  1. Go to Gmail Settings → Advanced → Enable "Templates"
  2. Compose a new email with your template content and signature
  3. Click the three dots → Templates → Save draft as template → Save new template
  4. Name it (like "Customer Inquiry Response") → Save
  5. When needed: Compose → three dots → Templates → choose your template

6. Email Best Practices Checklist

  • Use a single-column layout — easier to read on phones
  • Keep subject lines under 50 characters
  • Include alt text on all images: alt="PRML Records Logo"
  • Test every email on your phone before sending to a list
  • Always include your phone number (770-686-7726) and website
  • Add an unsubscribe link to mass emails (required by law)
  • Include your business address in mass emails (CAN-SPAM requirement)
  • Do not use all caps in subject lines — triggers spam filters
  • Check dark mode — test your signature on iPhone in dark mode
  • Remove old promotions — do not send emails referencing past events
  • Use plain text fallback for HTML emails
  • Avoid spam trigger words: FREE, GUARANTEED, URGENT, ACT NOW
