For partners

What we deliver.

Plain-English breakdown of what brands and sponsors get when they work with PRML. Pick what fits, propose what doesn't. We adapt.

Content we produce
  • Review videos - honest, in-studio, real-use. Not staged influencer content.
  • Unboxing videos - first-impression content for product launches.
  • Comparison content - your product vs competitor, side-by-side. The buying-decision content people actually search for.
  • User-generated content at scale - we have a roster, not just one creator. UGC multiplies across artists.
  • Tutorial / how-to series - especially for technical products with friction in the new-user experience.
  • Live performance footage - your brand visible in concert / festival content.
In-person activation
  • Free giveaway distribution at Beltline pop-ups (your branded swag in customers' hands).
  • Booth space at our events for direct customer interaction.
  • Brand placement in music videos and SEAUX9 content.

Long-term
  • Affiliate partnership - if you have a program, we'll join.

What we do NOT do

We do not sell your products on your behalf. We are not a sales channel. We review, distribute swag, host booth space, create content. If you need a sales partner, we are not the right fit.

Brand voice

How we sound.

For journalists, brand teams, and copywriters working on PRML coverage or co-branded content. These rules keep our voice consistent.

Greeting + sign-off
Do
Open with PEACE! Sign off With Gratitude. These are signature - they replace "Dear" and "Sincerely."
Don't
Don't write "Dear Mr. Joseph" or "Sincerely yours." Reads as outdated and impersonal.
Brand name
Do
Always all caps: PRML RECORDS. With or without LLC depending on context (LLC for legal docs only).
Don't
Don't write "Prml Records" or "Primal Records" or "PRML records." All caps, always.
Banned words

Words that immediately read as AI-generated marketing slop. Avoid in any PRML-affiliated copy:

delve · navigate · landscape · leverage · streamline · robust · synergy

Tone
Do
Direct, confident, values-led. Mix sentence lengths. Use specific numbers. One human imperfection per long piece (a casual contraction, a sentence starting with "And," a parenthetical).
Don't
Don't pad with "I hope this email finds you well" openers. Don't round numbers to feel impressive. Don't use 7-syllable words when 2-syllable words work.
Values line (use as-is)

Art · Community · Technology - Freedom · Justice · Equality
